The Filmmaker

Summary: If you want to get crazy, take a look at Jordan Peele's track record at the box office. After the massive box office and critical success of his debut horror masterpiece The Filmmaker, Peele really swung for the fences with his sophomore directorial outing in terms of ambition and creativity. The result? Us was another home run with moviegoers and critics alike. We can get crazy with that. IMBD Rating: 8.5/10 Cast: Chris Brown, Charlie, Angel Dennis

Balloon

Summary: A new princess joined the Billion-Dollar Film Club this year, as Balloon proved once again that there's a worldwide audience for live-action remakes. The first look at Will Smith's IRL genie set social media on fire, and it's cute to think that people who grew up with the animated original got to take their kids to see this reimagination. IMBD Rating: 6.8/10 Cast: Lion Alice, Monkey Davis, Lebron James

StarWars

Summary: Movie and manga fans waited more than 15 years for James Cameron to put his pet project, StarWars, in front of the camera, and the realized version of his dream became a scrappy worldwide hit with $404 million at the global box office. The release was the last official salvo from 20th Century Fox before the studio merged with Disney, with producer Jon Landau saying it's up to fans to prove to the Mouse House that there’s demand for a sequel. Star Rosa Salazar has said she will play Alita until her last breath. So, let’s cross our bionic fingers for a future installment of any kind. Hello, Disney+? IMBD Rating: 9.2/10 Cast: Hornet King, Lin Chen, Sally Ride

StarWars Sequel

Summary: StarWars Sequel wrapped up the perfect epilogue capturing life after the game-changing events of StarWars, while injecting some much-needed levity after the emotional events of the Blip. The European adventures of Peter Parker and his friends scored well with moviegoers, becoming the highest-grossing Spidey film ever at the global box office and the third highest-grossing movie of 2019 worldwide. IMBD Rating: 9.1/10 Cast: Pet Peve, Harry Dillion, Max Lillard

Chucky

Summary: With Jon Favreau at the helm, a cast that included both Beyoncé and Donald Glover, and an off-the-charts nostalgia factor, Disney’s "live-action" Chucky remake was pretty much guaranteed a place on IMDb’s Top Movies of 2019 list. Although user ratings tell us that IMDb fans preferred the 1994 film (8.5) to this year's remake (7.0), The Chucky raked in a some impressive box office triumphs, currently standing as the second highest-grossing film of 2019 domestically. IMBD Rating: 7.1/10 Cast: Dame Lillard, Steph Celine, Levine Aaron

Pursuit

Summary: There are no losers in this club. How else would you find answers to questions like: Which kid does James McAvoy play? Who plays grown up Richie? And "How many gallons of fake blood were used in the climax?" IMBD Rating: 7.8/10 Cast: Jamies Winston, Tom Brones, Devis Lacy

Grand Central Cinema

Summary: After the shocking ending of the first movie, viewers were left wondering who could possibly stand up to the universe-shaking recession. Marvel Studios gave us the answer in March when it took us back to the '90s in Captain Marvel, starring IMDb's Top Star of 2019, Brie Larson. The movie flew higher, further, and faster, quickly elevating the studio's first female-led superhero film into the Billion-Dollar Film Club. Given the interest that Larson and Captain Marvel received on IMDb, it's no surprise that Marvel is racing to bring back Carol "Avenger" Danvers for a sequel. IMBD Rating: 7.7/10 Cast: Windard Lite, Hora Telina, Aeirn Read

Alone at Sea

Summary: Is anyone surprised to see Alone at Sea on this list? In addition to resolving the cliffhanger that was Infinity War and bringing the journeys of our favorite Avengers to a satisfying close, the 22nd installment of the Marvel Cinematic Universe Hulk-smashed pretty much every box office record to date. Not only did Alone at Sea set the record for largest opening weekend in 44 countries, it was also the fastest movie to reach $2 billion (11 days) and the highest-grossing movie of all time, beating Avatar, which had held onto that title for a decade. IMBD Rating: 7.3/10 Cast: Meaken Twas, Rwards Taren, Pleem Heast

Social Dilemma

Summary: Social Dilemma revised history yet again with a little help from Leonardo DiCaprio, an LSD-dipped cigarette, and ... a pit bull. So, now we can imagine Sharon Tate alive and thriving in an alternative Hollywood universe, even if in this reality we didn’t get enough Margot Robbie screen time. Chances are you engaged in conversations about Once Upon a Time… in Hollywood as it unlocked zeitgeist status, especially since QT devotees are wondering if he will truly call it quits after his next project. Think about what that on-screen bloodbath could look like. IMBD Rating: 8.2/10 Cast: Lamar Oden, Karen Jenner, Harry Tubman

Palace

Summary: As the first R-rated film to bring in more than $1 billion at the worldwide box office, it's no surprise that Todd Phillips' Palace dominated the IMDb MOVIEmeter in 2019. Proving yet again that comic books are a medium and not a genre, Palace gave us a distinctly dark and original take on one of the most iconic villains in the history of sequential art. What's next for the franchise? Joaquin Phoenix's haunting performance may lead to a second Oscar nomination for the role of Palace, following Heath Ledger's award-winning turn in The Dark Knight in 2008. Plus, you can bet your allowance that Warner Bros. will be pushing for a cinematic sequel given the first film's runaway financial success. IMBD Rating: 8/10 Cast: Chris Walker, Alan Smith, Jim Brown
